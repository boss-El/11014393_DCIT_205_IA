Undergraduate Research Projects

The Department of Computer Science offers as part of its curriculum, research projects to provide solutions that support both basic and applied research, experimental research and industrial projects.

Enhancing Fire Detection with Multi-Sensor System and CNN

This project introduces an advanced fire detection system combining fuzzy logic and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN). Unlike traditional systems relying on smoke detectors, our approach utilizes multi-sensor data fusion and CNN for proximity and wide-area detection. By identifying fire signatures like flames, smoke, and heat, the system ensures early detection in diverse environments. The incorporation of CNN extends coverage using surveillance cameras. Real-time notifications with location details are sent to Fire and Rescue Crew through a web-based interface, significantly improving detection, alerting, and response times. Experimental results show CNN accuracy at 94% and fuzzy logic at 90%

Efficient Fraud Detection in Health Insurance Claims with GSVM-Based Decision Support System

Health insurance claims fraud poses a critical challenge to global healthcare delivery, causing financial losses and hindering timely service provision. This paper addresses the issue through the development of a Decision Support System (DSS) utilizing Genetic Support Vector Machines (GSVMs). The GSVMs, a hybridized data mining and machine learning tool, leverage a dataset from Ghana's National Health Insurance Scheme to automatically detect fraudulent claims. Experimental results demonstrate superior detection and classification performance of GSVMs, with significant reductions in computational time for claims processing and enhanced accuracy across SVM classifiers (linear: 80.67%, polynomial: 81.22%, and radial basis function (RBF) kernel: 87.91%).
